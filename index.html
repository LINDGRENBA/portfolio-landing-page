About Me:

I am a firm believer that anyone can accomplish anything if they work hard enough and are given the right resources and support. In spite of a rough introduction to science and technology in high school, I have learned to work hard at understanding each of these subjects and now have a deep, nerdy love for them. I enjoy listening to podcasts and books on tape while cooking, baking or walking my dog. I combine my love of learning and podcasts by listening to 'Time Suck' (NSFW!), an informational podcast that explores a vast range of subjects. Although the average human body is made up of more than 50% water, I'm pretty sure for me it's coffee. Sweet, sweet coffee...

Education:

I am addicted to learning. I obtained my Associate of Arts Degree by the time I graduated high school, and that was only the beginning. After that I went on to study several languages and graduated from Western Washington University with my Bachelors in Japanese. After teaching in Japan, I completed and intensive program in New York, NY. and received SIT TESOL certification. Three years later I returned to school to get an Associate of Science degree and a license to practice dental hygiene. Now, I'm enrolled at Epicodus, a programming bootcamp in Portland, OR. I began learning to code at first as a hobby, a way to unwind after work as well as a way to bring creativity back into my life. When I was still coding several years later, I decided to make it my career. I'm grateful that because I have worn many hats, I bring a lot of different skills to the table!

Work Experience:

  • Dental Hygiene: From 2016 to 2020 I have been working as a dental hygienist in Portland, OR. and currently have an active dental hygiene license.
  • Concessions: To support myself while getting my dental hygiene degree, I worked at the Moreland theater selling tickets and serving beer and snacks. It was a blast!
  • Administration: I helped onboard incoming students at Kaplan International Centers in downtown Portland. I would also write letters of recommendation, help students apply for renewal of funds from their governments, scanned many documents and was the student activities coordinator.
  • TESOL Instructor: I instructed students from all over the world who had come to Kaplan International Center to learn English as their second language.
  • TESOL Instructor: Working in Izumo, Japan as an English instructor was one of the most incredible experiences I have had in my life. I lived there for a little over a year and worked for AEON.
